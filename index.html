DIN-rail smart-home modules

Turn ordinary wall switches into a robust, local smart home.

OSELIA wires your 24 V switches back to compact DIN-rail modules, classifies every press, and publishes it to Home Assistant over MQTT — no cloud, no DNS, deterministic timing.

The modules

A gateway and its expander. Snap them onto the rail.

DI16-G gateway

OSELIA Hearth

The wired smart-home gateway on a DIN rail.

A 24 V wall-switch input gateway built on the RP2040-ETH. It reads up to 128 isolated inputs, classifies every press as single / double / long, and publishes to Home Assistant over MQTT — no cloud, no DNS, deterministic timing.

 DI-16X expander

OSELIA Ember

Sixteen more inputs, snapped onto the rail.

An input-expander module for the Hearth gateway. Each Ember adds 16 isolated 24 V inputs over the shared I²C field bus — chain up to seven to reach 128 inputs on a single gateway.

Why OSELIA

Built for installs that have to keep working.

Local-first, no cloud

Presses go straight to your MQTT broker on the LAN. No accounts, no DNS dependency, deterministic timing.

Native Home Assistant

A first-party OSELIA integration — event entities, device triggers, diagnostics, controls and OTA updates.

Industrial reliability

Opto-isolated 24 V inputs, hardware debounce, watchdog, auto-reconnect, and verified over-the-air firmware.

Scales on the rail

One Hearth gateway plus up to seven Ember expanders — 128 isolated inputs on a single MQTT device.
